# Page 4 — The Decision Engine

## Every stock gets one honest verdict

The engine weighs **19 independent signals** — trend, real buying pressure, company quality, institutional options activity, momentum, fear gauges, news tone — and produces:

| Output | What it means for the investor |
|---|---|
| **Verdict** | Buy stock, buy options, skip, or watch — with the trade structure chosen for current conditions |
| **Confidence** | How strongly the evidence agrees — sizing follows confidence, not excitement |
| **Entry / target / stop** | The full trade, priced before commitment |
| **Position size** | Calculated from a fixed 2% risk budget per trade |
| **The reasoning** | Every driver and warning listed — auditable, not a black box |

**"Skip" and "watch" are first-class answers.** The engine prevents more losses by refusing ambiguous setups than it earns by finding good ones.

# Page 6 — Disciplined Exits, Automated

## The exit is designed at entry — then it runs itself

**Profit-taking ladders.** Each holding gets a ladder of sell targets calibrated to *that stock's own volatility* — not arbitrary round numbers. As price climbs each rung, an alert fires with the exact shares to sell.

**Bail triggers.** Every position carries a pre-stated invalidation level requiring multi-signal confirmation — one bad tick doesn't shake the position; a broken thesis does.

**Always-on monitoring.** Every day, automatically:

- Full portfolio health report with charts and returns
- Alerts when any holding violates its trend, falls below cost, or hits a ladder rung
- Notifications delivered to Discord — the system finds *you*

> **Result in practice:** the discipline to sell 60% of a winning options position into strength — then exit the rest when the bail trigger fired — turned a trade that buy-and-hold would have closed at +34% into **+112%**.
