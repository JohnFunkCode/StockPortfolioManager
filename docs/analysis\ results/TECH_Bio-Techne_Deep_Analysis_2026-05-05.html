Key Lessons: Why the Capitulation Bottom Failed

1. Technicals Without Fundamentals = False Signals

The narrowing bid/ask spreads and extreme put positioning LOOKED like capitulation, but the underlying earnings were deteriorating. We had the technical setup right but the fundamental catalyst wrong. Don't chase bottoms in fundamentally broken stories.

2. The "Laggard" Thesis Was Predictive

TECH's -41 pp underperformance vs. sector was the biggest warning signal in the analysis. That gap exists for a reason: growth investors abandoned it months ago. The earnings report just confirmed what the market already knew.

3. Extreme Hedging (17:1 Put/Call) Wasn't Contrarian Here

Normally, extreme put hedging signals capitulation. But in a fundamentally deteriorating story, hedgers are correctly protecting themselves. The market was right to fear downside, even if the spike in hedging felt extreme.

4. No Higher-Low Pattern Was the Real Sell Signal

We noted "No higher-low pattern in recent swing lows — downtrend structure remains intact." This was the correct structural warning. Reversal patterns form FIRST; capitulation spreads narrow later. We had the order backwards.
